# Results Page — Context, Content, and Implementation Prompt

## Project context

Turnkey Marketing serves independent auto repair shops. The Results page is a sales and trust page for prospective customers who want concrete evidence that Turnkey can help a healthy or growing shop reach its next level.

The page should feel confident, specific, and credible. It should make the movement from “before Turnkey” to “today” easy to understand at a glance, while also explaining what changed in the marketing strategy. It is not an internal report and must not include account-manager email language, requests for information, or commentary written to Turnkey staff.

The page already contains three testimonial videos. Preserve those videos exactly as they are. Do not edit, replace, reorder, restyle, rename, move, remove, recompress, or rewrite the video elements, their sources, posters, captions, or testimonial data.

## Audience and conversion goal

- Primary audience: owners and operators of independent auto repair shops.
- Visitor mindset: skeptical but growth-oriented; looking for a marketing partner that understands shop economics.
- Primary goal: make the results believable and easy to scan, then encourage the visitor to book a consultation.
- Desired impression: Turnkey understands revenue, car count, ARO, retention, market position, and multi-location growth—not just clicks and impressions.

## Non-negotiable content rules

1. Lead with business outcomes, not marketing jargon.
2. Show the baseline, current result, percentage change, and timeframe whenever supplied.
3. Clearly label projections as projections.
4. Use “more than,” “over,” or “at least” where the supplied figure is open-ended.
5. Do not invent quotes, customer names, dates, locations, services, attribution, or intermediate monthly data.
6. Do not draw smooth trend charts from only two endpoints. A before/after comparison is more honest.
7. Avoid implying universal guarantees. Keep the existing client-results disclaimer or an equivalent concise statement.
8. Translate internal notes into prospect-facing copy. Do not mention an account manager, an email, or that someone was writing to Turnkey staff.
9. Keep every existing testimonial video untouched.
10. Use shop logos only from `/public/images/results/shop-logos/`. If a logo is missing, use a clean text fallback; do not fabricate a logo.

## Recommended page structure

### 1. Hero: immediate credibility

- Eyebrow: `Real shop results`
- Headline direction: `Growth you can see in the numbers.`
- Supporting copy direction: “See how independent repair shops increased revenue, car count, ARO, and capacity with a marketing strategy built around the business—not vanity metrics.”
- Include a compact disclaimer: “Client-specific examples. Results vary by shop, market, investment, and execution.”
- Use one to three verified aggregate callouts derived only from the approved cases. Prefer “6 shop growth stories,” “up to 118% revenue growth,” and “30%+ car-count growth” over invented averages.

### 2. Featured transformations

Lead with two or three high-impact, visually larger stories. Recommended:

- SSA European Auto Repair: $3.3M annual baseline to $7.2M projected.
- Torque European: $110K monthly to a consistent $220K–$230K.
- ASAP Auto Repair: simultaneous revenue and car-count growth.

Each featured story should include:

- Shop logo and name.
- Relationship start or timeframe.
- Large before → current metric treatment.
- Percentage increase.
- A concise outcome narrative.
- Two or three strategy tags such as `Google Ads`, `Postcards`, `Direct Track`, or `Marketing leadership` only where supplied.
- A clear `Projected` badge for projected figures.

### 3. Results grid

Show all six client stories in a responsive grid. Cards should be easy to compare but not forced into identical content. Use a shared information hierarchy:

1. Logo/name and relationship date.
2. Primary result.
3. Baseline → current/projected comparison.
4. Percentage change.
5. One short paragraph explaining the operational significance.
6. Secondary proof points where available: car count, ARO, consecutive records, new-customer share, team/capacity, or second-location growth.
7. Strategy tags.

Use honest before/after bars or paired number panels. Do not invent monthly trend values.

### 4. Video testimonials

Keep the complete existing video-testimonial section exactly as currently implemented. Do not touch its markup, copy, data, media paths, posters, order, sizing, controls, styling, or placement relative to its own heading and cards. The new case-study layout should be designed around this fixed section.

### 5. Additional proof

Existing written testimonials and independently verified proof may remain as supporting content after the six case studies. Reduce redundancy if necessary, but do not alter the videos. Keep distinct proof types clearly labeled: measured business results, video testimonials, owner quotes, and review growth.

### 6. CTA

- Headline direction: `What could the right marketing plan unlock for your shop?`
- Supporting copy: focus on a practical conversation about the shop’s current numbers and next growth target.
- Primary action: book a consultation using the project’s existing consultation URL.

## Visual direction

- Work within the existing Turnkey brand system and current Astro site architecture.
- Favor editorial case-study layouts, strong typography, spacious cards, crisp number comparisons, and restrained use of brand green, key blue, navy, charcoal, and off-white.
- Make the shop logo a trust cue, not the dominant visual.
- Do not use generic stock photos or fabricated dashboards.
- Keep data labels readable on mobile and avoid relying on color alone to communicate change.
- Use semantic headings and accessible alt text. Decorative icons should be hidden from assistive technology.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences for any reveal or hover treatment.
